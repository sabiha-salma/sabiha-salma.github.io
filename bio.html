About Myself


I am originally from Bangladesh, a small beautiful country located in South Asia on the Bay of Bengal. After having done with my higher secondary studies from Holy Cross College, I joined University of Dhaka (DU) in B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering program. I completed by undergrad studies in 2014. While at DU, I was exposed to a variety of research works in the field of Human Computer Interaction (HCI). That was when I felt an enthusiasm for research oriented higher studies. I started my Ph.D. in Computer Science program at George Mason University (GMU) in Fall 2018. Before coming to USA, I served as a Lecturer in Computer Science at State University of Bangladesh. Later in GMU, I had a different experience of teaching while working as a Graduate Teaching Assistant where I assisted the students from undergrad levels. Currently, I am working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the SAGE lab at GMU under the supervision of Dr. Kevin Moran. My research interests include Automated UI Analysis, HCI Considerations for Developer Tools, and AI for Software Engineering. Apart from my studies, I love to explore different places and hang out with friends. I love being closer to nature!
